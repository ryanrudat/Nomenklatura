# NOMENKLATURA: World-Building Bible

> **CRITICAL RULE:** This game is set in the **early 1950s**. No events, technologies, or references from after 1954 should appear. All historical events must be fictionalized and predate this period.

## THE PEOPLE'S SOCIALIST REPUBLIC OF VOLKERIA

### Overview
The People's Socialist Republic of Volkeria is a central superpower on the continent. It is NOT the Soviet Union, Russia, or any real country. All names, places, and events are fictional.

### Geography
- **Position:** Central superpower with a sliver of west coast access
- **East:** Unknown/unexplored territories (future expansion)
- **West:** Ocean access, trade routes
- **Surrounded by:** 8 neighboring nations of varying alignments

### Key Facts
- **Capital:** Volkhrad
- **Founding Year:** 1922 (The Revolution)
- **Government:** Single-party socialist state
- **Ruling Party:** The People's Worker Party (PWP)
- **Leader Title:** General Secretary

## DOMESTIC REGIONS OF VOLKERIA

### 1. Volkhrad Metropolitan District
- **Role:** Capital and political center
- **Population:** ~12 million
- **Character:** Seat of all central power - Presidium, ministries, security apparatus

### 2. Krovostok Industrial Combine
- **Role:** Heavy industry heartland
- **Name Meaning:** "Blood of the East"
- **Population:** ~23 million
- **Character:** Steel mills, coal mines, chemical plants

### 3. Chernograd Industrial Region
- **Role:** Manufacturing and finished goods
- **Name Meaning:** "The Black City"
- **Population:** ~15 million
- **Character:** Factory towns, tractor works, armament production

### 4. Vostochny Frontier
- **Role:** Eastern border defense zone
- **Name Meaning:** "Eastern Frontier"
- **Population:** ~8 million
- **Character:** Militarized border with Zimograd, permanent alert status

### 5. Zarechye Autonomous Region
- **Role:** Southern territories with distinct heritage
- **Name Meaning:** "Beyond the River"
- **Population:** ~12 million
- **Character:** Ancient cultures predating the Revolution, nominal autonomy

### 6. Baltika Territories
- **Role:** Western coastal region
- **Population:** ~6 million
- **Character:** Historic ports, intense surveillance, lingering resistance
- **Port Cities:** Kalthafen, Severnomorsk, Gradova

### 7. Severlag Territory
- **Role:** Frozen north, labor camp region
- **Name Meaning:** "Northern Camp"
- **Population:** ~4 million (including prisoners)
- **Character:** Permafrost, labor camps, mineral extraction

## NEIGHBORING NATIONS (8)

### SOCIALIST BLOC

#### 1. VALDORIA - Loyal Satellite State
- **Type:** Reliable socialist ally
- **Location:** Northeastern border
- **Population:** ~18 million
- **Character:** Industrial powerhouse, model socialist state, genuinely committed to the cause
- **Government:** People's Republic under Workers' Party leadership
- **Relationship:** Volkeria's most trusted partner, provides industrial goods, hosts military bases
- **Dynamic potential:** Could drift toward independence if Volkeria shows weakness
- **Key Feature:** Most developed satellite, showcase of socialist success

#### 2. TARNOVA - Restless Satellite State
- **Type:** Troublesome occupied territory
- **Location:** Southern border
- **Population:** ~25 million
- **Character:** Proud nation with deep religious traditions, accepts socialist government but resents domination
- **Government:** People's Republic (tightly controlled)
- **Relationship:** Periodic unrest, requires heavy surveillance, strategically important
- **Dynamic potential:** Could rebel, could be crushed, could negotiate more autonomy
- **Key Feature:** Religious underground, periodic "disturbances"

#### 3. ZIMOGRAD - Ideologically-Split Former Ally
- **Type:** Socialist rival
- **Location:** Northern/northeastern border
- **Population:** ~30 million
- **Character:** Broke from Volkeria's leadership over doctrinal disputes in 1949, charts independent socialist path
- **Government:** Socialist Republic (independent)
- **Relationship:** Former brothers now bitter rivals, competing for ideological leadership
- **Dynamic potential:** Could reconcile, could escalate to military confrontation
- **Key Feature:** The "Doctrinal Divergence" created permanent tension

- **Type:** Western-aligned democracy

- **Population:** ~20 million
- **Character:** Market economy, parliamentary democracy, partially backed by Atlantic Union
- **Government:** Parliamentary Republic
- **Relationship:** Hostile, border tensions, espionage war

- **Population:** ~15 million

- **Type:** Parliamentary monarchy
- **Location:** Offshore in western waters
- **Population:** ~40 million
- **Character:** Island nation with ancient parliament and constitutional monarch, naval power, fading imperial glory
- **Government:** Constitutional Monarchy with Parliament
- **Relationship:** Part of Western bloc, traditional great power, maintains global influence through navy
- **Dynamic potential:** Could face internal upheaval, monarchy could fall, colonial remnants could rebel
- **Key Feature:** Naval supremacy, intelligence services, "the island that never sleeps"

### OTHER

#### 7. THE FEDERATED STATES - Neutral Trading Conglomerate
- **Type:** Neutral trading bloc
- **Location:** Southern border, controls access to major waterway
- **Population:** ~12 million (across member states)
- **Character:** Conglomerate of smaller states formed after the War of Succession, united by trade interests
- **Government:** Federal Council (rotating presidency among member states)
- **Relationship:** Plays both sides, controls vital shipping lanes, profitable neutrality
- **Dynamic potential:** Could be pulled into either bloc, internal politics volatile
- **Key Feature:** Controls the Southern Waterway connecting inland sea to western ocean
- **History:** Formed in 1923 when smaller kingdoms united after their war of succession ended

#### 8. MARZOVIA - Monarchist Remnant
- **Type:** Ancient enemy, unreformed monarchy
- **Location:** Northwestern border
- **Population:** ~22 million
- **Character:** Old empire that never fully modernized, centuries of conflict with Volkeria's predecessor state
- **Government:** Absolute Monarchy (the Tsar still rules)
- **Relationship:** Deep-seated mistrust, territorial disputes, cultural animosity predating the Revolution
- **Dynamic potential:** Could modernize (capitalist or socialist revolution), could remain frozen in past
- **Key Feature:** The "eternal enemy" - wars between the two go back 400 years

## DISTANT POWER

### THE ATLANTIC UNION
- **Type:** Overseas superpower coalition
- **Location:** Across the western ocean
- **Population:** ~200 million (combined)
- **Character:** Wealthy, democratic, interventionist, leads the capitalist world
- **Government:** Federal democratic republic (dominant member) + allied democracies
- **Relationship:** Partially backs capitalist neighbors (Korvath, Brechtland, Commonwealth) but backing can change
- **Presence:** Mentioned in lore, influences events but doesn't border Volkeria directly
- **Key Feature:** Economic powerhouse, nuclear armed, fights cold war through proxies and aid

## DYNAMIC ALLIANCE SYSTEM

### Core Principle
**Relationships are fluid.** Revolutions can happen anywhere. Alignments can shift. The Atlantic Union's backing is partial and conditional. Nothing is permanent.

### Potential Events
- Socialist revolutions in capitalist states
- Counter-revolutions in satellite states
- Coups and leadership changes
- Alliance shifts and betrayals
- Economic crises rippling across borders
- Border incidents creating crises
- Atlantic Union intervention decisions

## HISTORICAL EVENTS (All Pre-1954)

### The Revolution (1922)
The founding event. Workers and soldiers overthrew the monarchy, establishing the People's Socialist Republic.

### The Revolutionary Period (1922-1928)
Consolidation of Party rule, suppression of rivals, foreign intervention repelled.

### The Great Purge (1936-1938)
Mass political repression. Hundreds of thousands arrested or executed.

### The Great Patriotic War (1941-1945)
Devastating war against fascist invasion. Tens of millions died. Victory established Volkeria as superpower.

### The Tarnova Disturbances (1948)
Worker unrest in Tarnova suppressed with "fraternal assistance."

### The Doctrinal Divergence (1949)
Zimograd expelled from socialist bloc for refusing Volkeria's leadership.

### The War of Succession (1918-1923)
Civil conflict in southern kingdoms that led to formation of the Federated States.

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

### Volkerian Names
- Slavic-sounding but fictional
- Examples: Brenner, Volkov, Ozols, Kovalenko, Petrov

### Valdorian Names
- Germanic/Slavic blend
- Examples: Ulmann, Hartmann, Weber

### Tarnovian Names
- Slavic with religious overtones
- Examples: Kowalski, Jaroszek, Stanislav

### Marzovian Names
- Old Slavic, aristocratic
- Examples: Romanov, Volkonsky, Obolensky

### Commonwealth Names
- Anglo-inspired
- Examples: Whitmore, Ashford, Pemberton
